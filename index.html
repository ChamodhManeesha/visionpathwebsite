Literature Survey

Project Scope

The visually impaired community faces significant challenges in accessing education and employment opportunities due to barriers related to accessibility and accommodation. In recent years, technological advancements have played a crucial role in addressing these challenges by offering innovative solutions tailored to the unique needs of visually impaired individuals.This literature review examines existing research and developments in this domain, focusing on an innovative application designed specifically for blind individuals. "Vision Path" integrates various features aimed at enhancing their skills, employability, and overall quality of life. Perkins eLearning is an online educational platform affiliated with the Perkins School for the Blind which is located in Watertown, Massachusetts, USA. The school is a renowned institution in the field of education for visually impaired people. The platform provides resources, training and support for blind or visually impaired individuals as well as for professionals, educators and families who work with and support these individuals. The platform offers a wide range of resources and services focused on improving the educational and personal development of visually impaired individuals.[1] Courser – founded in 2012 by two Stanford University professors, Andrew Ng and Daphne Koller, is an online learning platform that offers a wide range of courses, specializations and degrees from top universities and institutions around the world. The goal of developing the platform was making high-quality education more accessible to a global audience. [2] Learning Ally is a nonprofit organization that primarily focuses on providing support and resources for people with dyslexia and other reading impairments than it does on helping visually impaired people. Although some of their audio books and resources may still be useful to those who are blind or visually impaired, depending on their specific needs and preferences. [3] Accessibility is a major issue for visually impaired individuals. Studies by Nielsen (2006) and W3C (Web Accessibility Initiative)[4] highlight the importance of designing digital platforms that fulfill accessibility requirements so that the users with visual impairments can effectively interact and explore the materials easily. Online learning has become more popular due to its accessibility and flexibility. Research by Allen and Seaman (2014)[5] highlights the growth of online education, suggesting its ability to create inclusive learning environments. However, there are barriers to accessibility, which emphasizes the need for platforms that support visually impaired users. One fundamental aspect of the application "Vision Path" is its focus on providing suitable courses to improve the skills of visually impaired users. Through real-time emotion capture during training sessions, the system can gauge the user's level of understanding, thereby customizing the learning experience. This approach aligns with research by Cruz et al. (2019) [6], which suggests that personalized learning experiences significantly enhance learning outcomes for individuals with disabilities. By analyzing facial expressions, the application ensures that content delivery is tailored to the user's comprehension level, thereby fostering a more efficient learning process. Applications for facial expression recognition can be found in various domains, including education. The “Facial Action Coding System” (FACS), first introduced by Ekman (1992) [7] is the foundation for emotion recognition technology. In education, facial expression recognition has an effective way to monitor students’ level of engagement and emotional states during lessons (D’Mello & Graesser, 2012) [8]. Through the adaptation of this technology, visually impaired users can be provided real time feedback. Ethical concerns may arise when using facial expression recognition and other data driven features. Privacy issues, data security and consent are important factors that must be taken into consideration (Floridi et al., 2018)[9]. Ensuring users’ privacy and obtaining informed consent should be essential to the design of the application. Quizzes, exercises and progress tracking increase user engagement and motivation (Deterding et al.,2011)[10]. Adding those to the platform can create an engaging learning environment that motivates visually impaired users to actively participate. The application "Vision Path" goes beyond learning by incorporating features dedicated to career development and job placement. By offering a CV generation platform specifically designed for visually impaired individuals, the application addresses a critical barrier to employment. Research by Goggin and Newell (2013)[11] emphasizes the importance of accessible job-seeking tools for individuals with disabilities, highlighting the need for tailored solutions. Furthermore, the personalized job recommendation system within the application aligns with findings from Li et al. (2020)[12], indicating that matching job opportunities to individual skills and preferences significantly increases employment outcomes for visually impaired individuals. An innovative aspect of the application is its integration of virtual interview sessions for assessment and evaluation purposes. By harnessing technologies such as natural language processing and speech-to-text, the system enables visually impaired users to participate in virtual interviews seamlessly. This feature addresses the challenges faced by blind individuals in traditional interview settings, as highlighted by research by Hersh and Johnson (2012)[13]. The literature review shows that although there is existing research on accessible education technologies, there is still a gap in specifically designed platforms for people with visual impairments. The development of an application tailored to the needs of visually impaired individuals represents a significant advancement in accessibility and empowerment. By integrating features such as personalized learning, career development tools, and virtual interview sessions, the application addresses key challenges faced by this community. Drawing on insights from existing research, this literature review underscores the importance of customized solutions in enhancing the skills, employability, and overall quality of life for visually impaired individuals. Moving forward, further research and development in this area hold promise for continued advancements in accessibility and inclusion.

Research Objectives

Learning Web app for visually impaired, tracks understanding via facial expressions.

Design a web application for visually impaired users, integrating emotion recognition to capture feedback. It enables them to learn and develop skills from online sources while tracking their progress through facial expression analysis, ensuring effective and personalized lesson delivery.

Develop Web app helps blind individuals in job-seeking and career development.

Develop a web app to assist blind job seekers by scraping job postings from various online platforms. Use natural language processing to extract key details, machine learning to match skills with job requirements, and provide personalized recommendations based on user profiles and preferences.

Use NLP to craft quality resumes with relevant keywords.

Crafted accessible resume templates with NLP integration for seamless customization. Delivered tailored feedback and export features to streamline resume creation. Utilized keywords from job descriptions for accuracy. Achieved precise alignment with industry standards.

Leveraged virtual interviews for effective assessment and evaluation.

Incorporated virtual interview integration to harness its potential for assessment and evaluation. Seamlessly integrated platforms to facilitate comprehensive candidate evaluation, optimizing the hiring process for efficiency and effectiveness.

Methodology

\

The methodology employed in developing the Job Seeking and Training Application for Blind and Visually Impaired Individuals followed a systematic and iterative process, comprising several distinct stages. Initially, the Requirement Analysis phase involved meticulously identifying the indispensable features and functionalities essential for the application. This encompassed a comprehensive understanding of the specific requisites of blind and visually impaired users, encompassing accessibility features and adaptive technologies.Throughout the developmental journey, User Feedback Collection played a pivotal role. Actively seeking input from users ensured that the application resonated with their preferences and requirements. This feedback served as a cornerstone for refining features and augmenting overall usability

In Interface Design, paramount importance was given to crafting a user-friendly interface, meticulously tailored to the unique needs of visually impaired users. Integration of text-to-speech and speech-to-text functionalities was instrumental in enhancing accessibility and usability, thus fostering inclusivity within the application. The application seamlessly integrated Virtual Interview Simulations, providing users with a realistic platform to hone their interview skills. Real-time Natural Language Processing (NLP) analysis furnished personalized feedback on users' responses, thereby aiding in interview preparation and skill enhancement. To facilitate user progress tracking and identification of areas for improvement, Evaluation Marks were embedded within the application. This feature empowered users to monitor their performance over time, thus fostering continuous growth and development. Collaboration with Stakeholders, encompassing experts in accessibility and job training, proved invaluable. Their insights and guidance ensured adherence to industry standards and best practices, thus enriching the application's efficacy and relevance. User Engagement Initiatives, ranging from outreach programs to focus group discussions, were instrumental in fostering active participation and soliciting valuable feedback from users.Usability Testing, conducted with a representative sample of blind and visually impaired users, served as a vital checkpoint to identify and address any usability challenges promptly. Continual Development remained pivotal, ensuring the application's responsiveness to evolving user needs and emerging technological advancements. Updates and enhancements were diligently guided by effect evaluations and user feedback. In summation, this methodology was meticulously crafted to yield a robust and accessible platform, effectively catering to the distinctive requirements of blind and visually impaired individuals in their job seeking and training endeavors.

Research Problem & Solution

To effectively tackle the central research problem, it's crucial to adopt a multi-faceted approach that integrates technical innovation, accessibility enhancements, and user-centered design principles. This comprehensive strategy comprises several vital components. Firstly, technology advancement is key to developing an integrated job-seeking and training application. This involves seamlessly incorporating advanced features like speech-to-text, text-to-speech, natural language processing (NLP), and virtual interview/training sessions. Achieving this requires careful selection of suitable APIs or developing custom solutions to ensure accurate and smooth conversion between speech and text, as well as extracting meaningful insights from textual data.Concurrently, ensuring accessibility for individuals with vision impairments is paramount. It entails strict adherence to established accessibility standards such as WCAG and implementing features like screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation, high contrast modes, and alternative text for images. Additionally, providing customizable settings to cater to various levels of visual impairment and individual user preferences is essential to foster inclusivity and usability.Furthermore, adopting a user-centered design approach is critical in shaping the application's interface and functionality to meet the specific needs and preferences of users with vision impairments. Conducting thorough user research and usability testing within this demographic enables a deep understanding of their unique challenges, preferences, and pain points. This insight informs the creation of an intuitive, easy-to-navigate interface optimized for accessibility, while offering multiple interaction modalities such as voice commands and gestures to enhance the overall user experience. Moreover, the application should offer a comprehensive array of resources and tools for real-world interview preparation. This encompasses features like mock interview simulations, industry-specific practice questions, and guidance on effective communication and presentation skills. Access to informational resources such as resume/CV writing guides and career development articles further empowers users in their job-seeking endeavors.
